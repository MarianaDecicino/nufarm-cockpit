& Interview Preparations

In Progress
⬡ Analytical Foundation + Stakeholder List
Main Objective

To consolidate and analyze existing materials, learnings, and data regarding Carinata, establishing the project's analytical foundation for all subsequent phases.

Key Actions
  • Internal materials analysis
  • Preliminary ecosystem analysis
  • Structuring the project's analytical logic
  • Deep dive with Nufarm team (3–5 interviews)
  • Identification of priority themes for stakeholder interviews
  • Consolidation of list of growers and influencers for interviews
  • Validation of interview script (key themes)
Context & Key Pain Points Identified
Widespread Lack of Awareness
Massive knowledge gap regarding Carinata among growers and input suppliers (coops and dealers).
Competitive Perception
Concerns over area substitution and narrative conflicts with established crops like wheat, corn and canola.
Local Technical Validation
Lack of widely disseminated evidence on nematode control, biomass production, and yield benefits.
Market Access & Capillarity
Necessity to map entry points while navigating entrenched corn-centric market dynamics.
Logistics & Execution
Need for clear definitions on collection points, crushing arrangements, and commercial guarantees for growers and channels.

Applied Methodologies
Voice of Customer (VoC)

Qualitative methodology to consolidate perceptions, expectations, and adoption drivers. Key themes: key issues, main ambitions, reasons to be proud, demographic information.

Behavioral Analysis

Evaluation of behavioral factors influencing perception and decision-making. Key questions: where do you go for technical information? Where do you buy inputs? Where do you sell your production?

Stakeholder Mapping

Framework to identify key stakeholders and ecosystem dynamics that influence the Carinata journey — mapping influencers, decision-makers, and what accelerates or hinders adoption and trust.

05
Initial Hypotheses
H1
The main challenge is not agronomic — it is trust in the supply chain.
H2
Cooperatives have greater influence over adoption than Nufarm itself.
H3
The grower perceives commercial risk as higher than the potential economic return.
H4
There is low understanding of SAF and the final destination of the production.
H5
The decision to plant Carinata is strongly tied to the property's crop rotation logic.
H6
Growers with good technical results still have doubts about the future predictability of the chain.
